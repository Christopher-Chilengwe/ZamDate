Who we are Organizational Structure

The Diocese is normally administered by the mandate of Canon Law. The general organization of Chipata Diocese is not exempt from Canonical norms. As such, its Organizational structure is in such a way that at the helm of every activity, there is the Local Ordinary who is the Bishop. The Bishop is the overall head and custodian of the Local Church; the Father and Pastor. The Bishop does have some consultative bodies whose roles are purely consultative and not administrative. Such bodies are the College of Consultors, Presbyteral Council, Pastoral Council, Diocesan Council for the Laity, Catechists’ Council, and Associations of priests and Religious Men and Women. The Bishop’s role is to ensure that everything concerning the administration of the whole Diocese is properly coordinated and is directed in the way that will best achieve the good of that portion of the people of God entrusted to his care. (Can 472). In its pursuit to be effective and efficient, the Diocese is mainly organized through a team of management. This team falls under the Bishop and is appointed by him. It comprises the Vicar General, Pastoral Coordinator, Treasurer General and Bishop’s Secretary. This team does most of the administrative work of the Diocese. The Vicar General then, is the Chief Administrative Officer of the Diocese assisted by this team. The management team is responsible for coordinating and organizing the activities of respective departments, institutions, desks and commissions within the diocese. It is the link between the Bishop and relevant departments and institutions in pastoral and administrative activities. To ensure smooth operations, the departments, institutions and commissions closely collaborate with the team of management through their respective coordinator. From time to time, management meetings and decisions are effected on this level of command in the Diocese. The activities on the departmental level include social communications, education, health, gender programme, children’s programme, pastoral, peace, justice and developmental programme and projects. The diocese is organized in such a way that at the Diocesan level, all these various departments and institutions are interconnected through the management team and the overall custodian of the Diocese who is the Bishop. Particularly, the Catholic Diocese of Chipata has an Auxiliary Bishop who also in this case takes up the responsibility of the Diocesan Vicar General. At deanery level, deans play a very vital role in the pastoral administration of the diocese. Parish priests under the deans are practically in charge of the administration of the parishes which trickle down to outstations, Small Christian Communities and the family. .
